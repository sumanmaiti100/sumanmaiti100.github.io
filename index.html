Research Statement

My research interests lie at the intersection of cyber-physical systems security, formal methods, and safe reinforcement learning. I focus on developing rigorous vulnerability analysis and defense frameworks for safety-critical CPS domains such as smart grids, industrial control systems, and networked infrastructures, where adversaries can manipulate control commands, sensor measurements, actuator signals, or communication channels.

A central part of my work is the formal specification of safety and security requirements using temporal logic, particularly Metric Temporal Logic, to capture time-bounded constraints on system states, recovery behavior, operational limits, and sustained safe operation. I am also interested in using formal methods such as SMT solving, hybrid automata, robustness-guided falsification, and reachability analysis to systematically synthesize unsafe scenarios and verify whether learned controllers satisfy safety properties.

On the defense side, I work on safe reinforcement learning methods that train mitigation and protection agents under operational constraints, where the agent must restore the system to a safe region while avoiding unsafe or overly aggressive actions. My broader goal is to build mathematically grounded and practically deployable CPS security frameworks that combine temporal-logic-based specifications, formal verification, and safe RL to improve resilience against coordinated cyber-physical attacks.

Research Interests

My work spans several interconnected areas within cyber-physical systems security and formal methods.

CPS Security

Rigorous defense frameworks for safety-critical systems where adversaries manipulate control commands, sensor data, actuator signals, or communication channels.

Smart Grid Security

Vulnerability analysis and defense of power grid systems against load alteration attacks, false data injection, and coordinated cyber-physical intrusions on IEEE bus models.

Formal Methods & MTL

Safety and security requirements specified in Metric Temporal Logic; verification via SMT solving, hybrid automata, and reachability analysis to formally prove system safety.

Safe Reinforcement Learning

Training protection agents under operational safety constraints, ensuring the agent restores the system to a safe region without violating operational or physical limits.

SCADA & ICS Security

Security analysis of industrial control systems and SCADA environments using domain protocols (DNP3, DNP3-SA, Modbus) and formal threat modeling frameworks.

Attack Synthesis & Falsification

Systematic synthesis of adversarial inputs and unsafe scenarios using robustness-guided falsification (Staliro) to discover safety-critical vulnerabilities in CPS.

SCADA & Industrial Protocol Experience

A significant part of my CPS security research involves understanding and analyzing the industrial communication protocols that underpin SCADA systems and critical infrastructure. Hands-on experience with these protocols informs both my threat modeling and formal analysis of attack surfaces in real-world deployments.

DNP3

Distributed Network Protocol 3

DNP3 is the dominant communication protocol in electric utility SCADA systems. My work involves analyzing DNP3 traffic in smart grid scenarios, characterizing adversarial manipulation of data link and transport-layer frames, and developing formal threat models for DNP3-based substation deployments.

Electric Utilities Master–Outstation Architecture Smart Grid

DNP3-SA

DNP3 Secure Authentication

DNP3 Secure Authentication extends DNP3 with challenge-response authentication using HMAC-based mechanisms. My experience includes studying authentication sequence vulnerabilities, analyzing replay and man-in-the-middle attack scenarios, and evaluating its effectiveness in protecting critical infrastructure communications.

Cryptographic Authentication HMAC Critical Infrastructure

Modbus

Modbus RTU / Modbus TCP

Modbus is one of the oldest and most pervasive serial communication protocols in industrial automation and PLC integration. I have worked with Modbus in research settings to characterize its inherent security limitations—particularly the absence of authentication—and to build threat models for Modbus-connected field devices.

Industrial Automation PLC Integration Threat Modeling
